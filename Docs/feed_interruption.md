Feed-stopped-unexpectedly pathway (your headline safety net)
 
| Step | Action | Source |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | If a feed-related SC insulin dose is **due now, do NOT give it.** | JBDS §10; UHL App. B |
| 2 | Check CBG **hourly** (up to q15min if hypo signs). | JBDS §8.2, §10 |
| 3 | If feed-specific insulin **already given** and feed off **>30 min** → IV glucose to prevent hypo until restart. | UHL §2 (recs); JBDS §10 |
| 4 | This is where the **IOB guard** fires: any feed-related rapid/soluble insulin still active = active hypo-risk window. | UHL §2.9; JBDS §10 |
| 5 | T1/insulin deficiency: never omit basal; if NBM/off **>2 h** → ketosis risk → VRIII may be needed. | JBDS §6, §10; UHL §2.6 |
| 6 | On restart, give the **correct** feed-related dose (may differ from the missed one). Inform doctors; refer DIT if prolonged. | JBDS §10; UHL §2.9 |
 
The two real case studies in JBDS 05 are exactly these failures — a T1 patient whose basal was omitted and went into DKA, and a T2 patient given feed insulin after the feed was stopped who dropped to 1.9 mmol/L. Those make ideal demo scenarios.
